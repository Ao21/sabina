Sabina Egan

My work is based on the aesthetic quality of the hidden engineered components within structures. Through minimalist abstract paintings, the images display the artistic merit concealed beneath decorative facades. I have also assembled steel sculptures, with the purpose of imitating the buildings of a city skyline, thus creating the structural aesthetic of cities, on a smaller scale, through an artistic medium.

The materials I use are based on those utilised by the construction industry, namely plaster, concrete and steel. The surface quality gained from casting tiles of plaster allows for a variety of textures that are integral to the paintings themselves. My aim is to question the boundaries of art within engineering, and engineering within art and my paintings have various aspects of this work process.

I have gathered images from construction sites, and isolated specific elements to be painted onto plaster casts of various sizes. The outcome is a range of paintings highlighting an engineer’s contribution to an artist’s vision. The colour scheme and painting style used are based on blue/green and grey tones that are also reflective of the construction industry. The inclusion of horizon lines within my work allows for more depth within the paintings.

The two large scale pieces are site specific for this space. With the structural horizon on view, the window is a complementary feature to my paintings and I wished to highlight this by framing it with two door-like panels that contain a continuation of the horizon line at a certain eye-level. The mirrored images are based on the foundations that are necessary for the construction of any building.
